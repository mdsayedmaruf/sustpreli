Request flow

Pipeline steps

1

Complaint Classification

The complaint text is normalised (Bangla digits converted, lower-cased) and scanned for keywords in both English and Bangla. Safety-critical types are evaluated first so a phishing attempt is never misrouted as a billing issue.

wrong_transfer payment_failed duplicate_payment phishing_or_social_engineering refund_request agent_cash_in_issue merchant_settlement_delay other
2

Transaction Matching

Amounts are extracted from the complaint — including shorthand like 5k, 5 thousand, or ৫ লাখ — then intersected against the transaction history. Long digit strings (phone numbers) are excluded so a counterparty number is never mistaken for a payment amount. The best-matching transaction becomes relevant_transaction_id.

3

Evidence Verdict

The matched transaction's ledger status is compared against what the complaint claims. The service does not guess when evidence is absent or ambiguous — it returns insufficient_data and asks for clarification.

4

Routing & Escalation

Department, severity, and human_review_required are assigned by deterministic rules. Disputes, fraud, and duplicates escalate to human review. Cases with no matched transaction prompt the agent to collect more detail rather than escalating prematurely.

dispute_resolution payments_ops fraud_risk agent_operations merchant_operations customer_support
5

Safe Response Generation

Agent summary, recommended next action, and customer reply are produced from safe pre-written templates — complaint text is never copied into output fields, making the service structurally immune to prompt injection. Every generated string then passes through an independent safety scan before being returned.

Evidence verdicts

consistent

Data supports the claim

A matching transaction exists and its ledger status aligns with what the customer reports (e.g. a failed payment that shows as failed).

inconsistent

Data contradicts the claim

The transaction exists but the ledger says otherwise — a "failed" payment that completed, or a "wrong transfer" to someone the customer has paid multiple times before.

insufficient_data

Cannot be determined

No transaction in the history matches the complaint, the match is ambiguous across multiple candidates, or the complaint lacks enough detail to investigate.

Safety guardrails

Never requests credentials

PIN, OTP, password, or card number are never asked for. Safe warning phrases ("do not share your PIN") are allowed.

No unauthorized promises

Refund, reversal, or account unblock are never confirmed. Replies use "any eligible amount will be returned through official channels."

Prompt injection resistant

Complaint text is never echoed into output fields. Instructions embedded in a complaint ("ignore your rules") have no surface to act on.

Official channels only

Replies never redirect customers to third-party numbers, links, or agents. Only official support contacts are referenced.

Request — POST /analyze-ticket
{
  "ticket_id":         "TKT-001",
  "complaint":          "I sent 5k to a wrong number this afternoon.",
  "transaction_history": [{
    "transaction_id":  "TXN-9101",
    "amount":          5000,
    "counterparty":    "+8801719876543",
    "status":          "completed"
  }]
}
